=== Summary ===
Yes, ginseng (人参) may help with sleep, but primarily for specific types of insomnia related to qi deficiency patterns in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), such as heart-spleen deficiency or heart qi deficiency. It is not universally effective for all insomnia types and can worsen sleep if misused.

=== Key Points ===
- **Pattern-specific efficacy**: Ginseng is most effective for insomnia caused by heart qi deficiency, heart-spleen deficiency, or qi-yin deficiency, characterized by symptoms like dream-disturbed sleep, forgetfulness, and fatigue [dayi.org.cn](https://m.dayi.org.cn/qa/334518.html) [health.rednet.cn](https://health.rednet.cn/content/2020/08/06/7811169.html).
- **Mechanism**: It contains "安神益智" (calming the spirit and improving intelligence) properties that nourish heart blood and stabilize the spirit when deficiency patterns are present [dayi.org.cn](https://m.dayi.org.cn/qa/334518.html).
- **Contraindications**: Not suitable for insomnia accompanied by yin deficiency with fire (symptoms like flushed face, dry mouth, constipation), high blood pressure, or excess heat patterns [health.rednet.cn](https://health.rednet.cn/content/2020/08/06/7811169.html).
- **Risk of adverse effects**: Overuse or long-term use can cause nervous sensitivity, elevated blood pressure, or diarrhea, which may paradoxically worsen sleep quality [dayi.org.cn](https://m.dayi.org.cn/qa/334518.html).
- **Clinical formulations**: Compound preparations like Ginseng Spleen-Returning Pill (人参归脾丸) are specifically used for spleen-heart deficiency insomnia [familydoctor.com.cn](https://m.familydoctor.com.cn/201703/1803689.html).

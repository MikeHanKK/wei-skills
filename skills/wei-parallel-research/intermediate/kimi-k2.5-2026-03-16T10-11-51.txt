=== Summary ===
Yes, ginseng (人参) may help with sleep, but primarily for specific types of insomnia according to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) theory—particularly those caused by heart qi deficiency, heart-spleen deficiency, or qi-yin deficiency. It is not a universal sleep aid and can worsen sleep if used inappropriately or in excessive doses.

=== Key Points ===
- **TCM Indications**: Ginseng is traditionally used to treat insomnia caused by "heart qi deficiency" or "heart-spleen deficiency" patterns, characterized by symptoms like palpitations, forgetfulness, and dream-disturbed sleep [dayi.org.cn](https://m.dayi.org.cn/qa/334518.html) [health.rednet.cn](https://health.rednet.cn/content/2020/08/06/7811169.html).
- **Mechanism**: It is believed to "calm the spirit" (安神) and nourish heart blood, thereby improving sleep quality in deficiency patterns [familydoctor.com.cn](https://m.familydoctor.com.cn/201703/1803689.html).
- **Clinical Evidence**: A clinical study showed that "Shenwei Mixture" (containing ginseng) combined with herbal fumigation improved sleep scores in patients with qi-yin deficiency type insomnia [jstcm.cn](https://www.jstcm.cn/article/doi/10.19844/j.cnki.1672-397X.2021.02.011).
- **Contraindications**: Not suitable for insomnia accompanied by "yin deficiency with fire" (heat symptoms), high blood pressure, or nervous sensitivity; overdose may cause hypertension, nervousness, and diarrhea, thereby worsening sleep [dayi.org.cn](https://m.dayi.org.cn/qa/334518.html) [health.rednet.cn](https://health.rednet.cn/content/2020/08/06/7811169.html).

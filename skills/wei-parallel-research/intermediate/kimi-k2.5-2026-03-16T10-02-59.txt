=== Summary ===
Yes, ginseng (人参) may help with sleep, but primarily for specific types of insomnia according to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) theory—specifically those caused by heart-spleen deficiency (心脾两虚), heart qi deficiency (心气不足), or heart-kidney qi deficiency (心肾气虚). It is believed to work by tonifying qi and nourishing the heart, thereby calming the spirit (安神). However, it is not suitable for all insomnia types and may worsen sleep if used inappropriately or in excess.

=== Key Points ===
- **Specific Indications**: Ginseng is traditionally used for insomnia accompanied by symptoms of qi deficiency such as fatigue, palpitations, poor memory, and spleen weakness, rather than for all insomnia types.
- **Mechanism**: It tonifies primordial qi (元气) and heart qi, which nourishes the spirit (心神) and calms the mind; it is often formulated into compounds like Renshen Guipi Wan (人参归脾丸) for this purpose.
- **Contraindications**: Not recommended for those with yin deficiency, high blood pressure, history of cerebral hemorrhage, or symptoms like flushed face, dry mouth, and constipation; overdose may cause nervousness, elevated blood pressure, and diarrhea, potentially worsening sleep quality.

=== Summary ===
Ginseng may improve sleep quality, but primarily for specific types of insomnia associated with qi deficiency patterns in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), such as heart-spleen deficiency or heart-kidney qi deficiency. It is not universally effective for all insomnia types, and improper use can actually worsen sleep.

=== Key Points ===
- TCM-specific efficacy: Most effective for insomnia caused by heart qi deficiency, heart-spleen deficiency, or qi-yin deficiency, typically presenting with fatigue, palpitations, and dream-disturbed sleep
- Risk of adverse effects: Overdose or long-term use may cause hypertension, nervous sensitivity, and diarrhea, potentially exacerbating sleep problems rather than improving them
- Clinical formulations: Compound preparations like Ginseng Guipi Pill (人参归脾丸) are commonly used for spleen-heart deficiency insomnia, while single herb use requires careful dosing (typically 1–3g daily)
- Research limitations: While TCM clinical studies support benefits for specific deficiency patterns, high-quality evidence for general insomnia treatment remains limited

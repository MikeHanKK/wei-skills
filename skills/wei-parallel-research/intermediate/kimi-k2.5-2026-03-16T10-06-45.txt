=== Summary ===
Ginseng may help with sleep, but primarily for specific types of insomnia associated with qi deficiency patterns in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), such as heart-spleen deficiency or heart qi deficiency. It is not universally beneficial and can worsen sleep quality if used inappropriately.

=== Key Points ===
- **Specific TCM indications**: Ginseng is traditionally used to treat insomnia caused by heart qi deficiency, heart-spleen deficiency, or qi-yin deficiency by tonifying qi, generating blood, and calming the spirit (安神益智).
- **Contraindications and risks**: It is unsuitable for insomnia related to yin deficiency with fire (symptoms like red face, dry mouth, constipation) or for individuals with high blood pressure; excessive doses may cause nervousness, elevated blood pressure, and paradoxically worsen sleep.
- **Preparation matters**: Common effective forms include ginseng powder (2-3g daily), sliced ginseng tea (3-5g), or prepared formulations like Ginseng Guipi Pill (人参归脾丸) specifically for spleen-heart deficiency patterns.

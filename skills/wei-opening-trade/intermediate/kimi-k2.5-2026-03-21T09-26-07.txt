=== Summary ===
According to reports, the Trump administration is considering occupying or blockading Kharg Island—which handles approximately 90% of Iran's crude oil exports—to pressure Tehran into reopening the Strait of Hormuz. While controlling the island would sever Iran's primary economic lifeline and provide significant leverage, analysts note there is no guarantee this would compel Iran to unblock the strait. The move would require ground troops and risks triggering Iranian retaliation against regional oil facilities, potentially escalating rather than resolving the crisis.

=== Key Points ===
- Kharg Island is Iran's energy artery, processing ~90% of its crude exports; controlling it would "cut off the regime's lifeline"
- The strategy aims to create leverage for negotiations, but success is uncertain—occupation does not guarantee Tehran will capitulate or reopen the strait
- Implementation would require amphibious assault/ground troops (US Marines are reportedly being deployed), representing a major escalation beyond airstrikes
- High risk of Iranian retaliation against Saudi, UAE, or other regional oil infrastructure, potentially causing wider supply disruptions
- Alternative approaches being considered include weakening Iran via sustained airstrikes followed by naval escorts for tankers, avoiding invasion
